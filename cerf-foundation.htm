CENTRAL

One fund for the entire UN system.

In the chaos after a disaster strikes, having a central fund that has already collected donations from over 120 countries means more lives are saved. The UN’s Central Emergency Response Fund works across all UN agencies and humanitarian partners on the ground to provide the most cost-effective assistance, prioritized by need and impact. The flexibility to respond in the best way needed means each emergency has funds immediately available, delivered in a transparent way. That is the power of a CENTRAL fund to save lives.

When hundreds of thousands of lives were lost, and millions more ruined, by the tsunami that hit South East Asia in 2004, one lesson was clear: by the time the world responds to a crisis with humanitarian aid, for many it is sadly too late. What was needed was a pool of emergency funds, ready *before *the disaster strikes. That’s why the world came together to create the UN’s Central Emergency Response Fund.

Each year, the vast majority of countries on earth contribute money to a central piggy bank, so funds are ready to be used as soon as they are needed.

That money doesn’t care what ethnicity the people are, what country they live in, whether it’s a ‘celebrity’ cause, or whether the disaster was caused by humans or mother nature. That money only cares about who needs it most. That’s how the *UN’s Emergency Fund *is allocated: quickly, impartially, and very effectively. Just like every household should have a rainy day fund—*just in case* —so should the world have an insurance policy. Together, we saves lives by being prepared.

The UN Central Emergency Response Fund was an experiment when it started in 2006. Now, it is clear that it has been a resounding success. The organizations who carried out the work are the frontline heroes fueled by the *UN’s Emergency Fund, *which in turn is fueled by the donors. The people of the world have a central fund to help each other at their time of greatest need. The UN is saving lives, powered by the Central Emergency Response Fund.
